PENNY

DREADFUL

The penny dreadful was a
  19th-century
publishing phenomenon.

They were
cheap, sensational, highly

illustrated stories

so popular with the Victorian public.

‘Penny bloods’ was the original name for the booklets that, in the

1860s, were renamed penny dreadfuls

and told stories of adventure, initially of

piratesand highwaymen,

Later concentrating on crime and detection. Issued

weekly.

Each ‘number’, or episode, was  eight-sixteen pages,

with a black-and-white illustration on the top half of the front page.

Later penny-bloods found success with stories of
true crimes.
The most successful of them was the story of
Sweeney Todd,
Demon Barber.

PENNY

Dreadful

Penny Dreadful   is an American-British

horror-thriller
fantasy television series produced for

Showtime.

The series features various folk horror characters: vampires, werewolves, witches, the living dead.
